Grilled Cucumber and Mushroom Bites

  • Time: 20 mins
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Cucumber 1
Mushroom About 24
Tomato 2
Lemon Juice 3-4 tbsp
Cilantro Handful
Mint Dozen
Balsamic Vinegar 2 tbsp
Honey 2 tbsp
Truffle Oil Garnish
Olive Oil To saute
Cheese Garnish
Cream Cheese 1 tbsp

Step 1: Cut the cucumber into half vertically. Take the pulp out of both halves.

Step 2: Cut the tomatoes into 4-6 pieces. Finely cut the cilantro and mint.

Step 3: Saute the tomatoes for 5-6 mins until golden.

Step 4: Add the tomatoes, mint, cilantro, balsamic vinegar, honey, some grated cheese and lemon juice into a blender, and blend until a creamy sauce is made.

Step 5: Saute the mushrooms in a pan with olive oil until they are deep brown and moist.

Step 6: Add the sauce into the hollow cucumuber. Grate a thick layer of cheese on the sauce. Place the mushrooms on top of the sauce.

Step 7: Turn on the griller. Place the cucumbers on the griller and grill until the cucumber is charred on the bottom.

Step 8: Place the dish on a plate and garnish with truffle oil and mint. Place some cream cheese on top of the dish. Grate some cheese on the top if desired.

Source: My Own Recipe

Falafel with Tzatziki

  • Time: 5 mins plus 50 mins plus one day
  • Serves: 24 patties and 2-3 cups of Tzatziki

Falafel

Ingredients Quantity
Dried chickpeas 2 cups
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Fresh parsley
leaves, stems
removed		 1 cup
Fresh cilantro
leaves, stems
removed		 3/4 cup
Fresh dill,
stems removed		 1/2 cup
Onion 1 small
Garlic cloves,
peeled		 7-8
Salt To taste
Ground black pepper 1 tbsp
Ground cumin 1 tbsp
Ground coriander 1 tbsp
Cayenne Pepper 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Toasted Sesame Seeds 2 tbsp
Oil For frying

Step 1: (One day in advance) Place the dried chickpeas and baking soda in a large bowl filled with water to cover the chickpeas by at least 2 inches. Soak overnight for 18 hours (longer if the chickpeas are still too hard). When ready, drain the chickpeas completely and pat them dry.

Step 2: Add the chickpeas, herbs, onions, garlic and spices to the large bowl of a blender fitted with a blade. Run the blender 40 seconds at a time until all is well combined forming a the falafel mixture.

Step 3:Transfer the falafel mixture to a container and cover tightly. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or (up to one whole night) until ready to cook.

Step 4: Just before frying, add the baking powder and sesame seeds to the falafel mixture and stir with a spoon.

Step 5: Scoop tablespoonfuls of the falafel mixture and form into patties (1/2 inch in thickness each). It helps to have wet hands as you form the patties.

Step 6: Fill a medium saucepan 3 inches up with oil. Heat the oil on medium-high until it bubbles softly. Carefully drop the falafel patties in the oil, let them fry for about 3 to 5 minutes or so until crispy and medium brown on the outside. Avoid crowding the falafel in the saucepan, fry them in batches if necessary.

Step 7:Place the fried falafel patties in a colander or plate lined with paper towels to drain.

Source: The Mediterranean Dish - Falafel

Creamy Roasted Cauliflower Soup

  • Time: 45 mins
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Head cauliflower cut into bite-size florets 1 large
Extra-virgin olive oil 3 tbsp
Fine sea salt to taste
Red onion 1 Medium
Garlic, pressed or minced 2 cloves
Vegetable broth 4 cups
Unsalted butter 2 tbsp
Ground nutmeg 1/4 tsp
Finely chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley, chives and/or green onions 2 tbsp to garnish

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit. If desired, line a large, rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup.

Step 2: On the baking sheet, toss the cauliflower with 2 tablespoons of the olive oil until lightly and evenly coated in oil. Arrange the cauliflower in a single layer and sprinkle lightly with salt. Bake until the cauliflower is tender and caramelized on the edges, 25 to 35 minutes, tossing halfway.

Step 3: Once the cauliflower is almost done, in a Dutch oven or soup pot, warm the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat until shimmering. Add the onion and ¼ teaspoon salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is softened and turning translucent, 5 to 7 minutes.

Step 4: Add the garlic and cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 30 seconds, then add the broth.

Step 5: Reserve 4 of the prettiest roasted cauliflower florets for garnish. Then transfer the remaining cauliflower to the pot. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat as necessary to maintain a gentle simmer. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 20 minutes, to give the flavors time to meld.

Step 6: Once the soup is done cooking, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, carefully transfer the hot soup to a blender, working in batches if necessary. (Do not fill past the maximum fill line or the soup could overflow!)

Step 7: Add the butter and blend until smooth. Add the lemon juice and nutmeg and blend again. Add additional salt, to taste (I usually add another ¼ to ¾ teaspoon, depending on the broth). This soup tastes amazing once it’s properly salted! You can also a little more lemon juice, if it needs more zing. Blend again.

Step 8: Top individual bowls of soup with 1 roasted cauliflower floret and a sprinkle of chopped parsley, green onion and/or chives. This soup keeps well in the refrigerator, covered, for about four days, or for several months in the freezer.

Source: Cookie and Kate - Creamy Roasted Cauliflower Soup

Pesto Pasta

  • Time: 25 mins
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Pine Nuts 1/2 cup
Basil 2 cups
Lemon Juice 2 tbsp
Garlic clove 1
Sea Salt 1/4 tsp
Grounded Pepper 1/3 tsp
Olive Oil 1/4 + 1/3 cup
Parmesan Cheese 1/4 cup
Spaghetti pasta 12 ounces

Step 1: In a blender, combine the pine nuts, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper and pulse until well chopped.

Step 2: Add the basil and pulse until combined.

Step 3:With the blender running, drizzle in the olive oil and pulse until combined. Add the Parmesan cheese, if using, and pulse to briefly combine. For a smoother pesto, add more olive oil.

Step 4: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Prepare the spaghetti according to the package instructions, cooking until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain.

Step 5: Transfer the pasta to a large skillet. Add the pesto and ½ cup of the starchy pasta water and toss to coat, adding more pasta water as needed to coat the pasta in a lightly creamy sauce. If desired, gently warm the pasta over low heat.

Step 6: Season to taste and serve topped with freshly grated Parmesan and lots of basil.

Source: Love and Lemons - Pesto Pasta

Dolma

  • Time: 2hrs 45 mins
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Grape Leaves 1 jar
Short-Grain Rice 2 cups
Tomato 2 large
Parsley 1 bunch
Onion 1 medium
Mint Leaves 1 cup
Olive Oil 3/4 cup
Lemons 3
Salt 3 tsp
Black Pepper 1/2 tsp
Potato 1 large

Step 1: If using jarred grape leaves, rinse the brine using cold water and set aside to drain any water.
For fresh grape leaves, boil for 2 minutes before rinsing. Set aside to cool before using.

Step 2: Prepare the filling for the grape leaves by chopping all your washed vegetables. Drain the excess juices from the chopped tomato.

Step 3:Wash the short-grained rice thoroughly until water runs clear. In a large bowl, add the finely chopped parsley, mint leaves, onion, tomato and rice.

Step 4: Juice 2 of the lemons and add to the filling mixture, along with ½ cup of olive oil, one teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of black pepper. Mix well to combine.

Step 5: Lay out the grape leaf with the vein-side up (shiny side down). Remove the stem if it is not already removed. Add 1-2 tablespoons of filling depending on the size of the grape leaf and arrange in a thin line. You want to leave at least 1 cm of room on each side. Be careful not to overfill.
Fold each side of the leaf over the filling beginning with the bottom side and roll tightly to prevent the leaf from opening up while cooking. Repeat until you have no more leaves left. If the grape leaf is very large, cut in half, then stuff and roll.

Step 6: Peel and wash the large potato and slice in 1 cm slices. Slice the medium tomato the same size. Lay the tomato slices first, then the potato slices on the bottom of a deep pot.
Arrange the stuffed grape leaves side by side and on top of each other over the potatoes. Sprinkle the remaining two teaspoons of salt over the grape leaves. Juice the remaining lemon and add it to the pot, along with the ¼ of olive oil remaining and leftover juices from the filling.

Step 7: Place a plate about 1-2 inches smaller in diameter than the pot, over the stuffed leaves. Fill the pot with enough water to cover all the leaves and up to the plate. Place a heavy item on top of the plate to prevent the grape leaves from opening up while cooking. Cook on medium heat for 30 minutes. Reduce the heat to low, and cover with a lid and cook for a remaining 45 minutes. To check if grape leaves are done, take a grape leaf out and try it!

Step 8: Once the grape leaves are done cooking and have cooled for at least 15 minutes, flip the pot over onto a serving platter. There should be a little bit of lemon water juices left. Serve warm or cold.

Source: Simply Leb - Dolma

Mushroom Risotto

  • Time: 45 mins
  • Serves: 4-6
Ingredients Quantity
Extra-virgin olive oil 4 tbsp
Mixed mushrooms, chopped 1 1/2 pounds
Sea salt ¾ teaspoon, plus more to taste
Freshly ground black pepper 1 tsp
Medium yellow onion, chopped 1
Garlic cloves, finely chopped 2
Fresh thyme leaves 1 tbsp
Uncooked Arborio rice, rinsed 1½ cups
Dry white wine 2/3 cups
Warmed vegetable broth 5 cups
Grated pecorino or Parmesan cheese 1/2 cup, plus more for serving
Chopped fresh parsley For garnish

Step 1: Heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil in a Dutch oven or large, deep skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms, ½ teaspoon of the salt, and several grinds of pepper and toss to coat. Cook, stirring only occasionally, for 8 minutes, or until soft and browned. Remove from the pan and set aside. Work in batches if necessary.

Step 2: Wipe out the pan and return it to the heat. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil, the onion, and the remaining ¼ teaspoon sea salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 to 8 minutes, or until softened. Stir in the garlic, thyme, and rice. Let cook for 1 minute, then add the wine. Stir and cook for 1 to 3 minutes, or until the wine cooks down.

Step 3: Add the broth ¾ cup at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition of broth to be absorbed before adding the next. With the final addition of broth, stir two-thirds of the sautéed mushrooms into the risotto. Cook until the risotto is creamy and the rice has a slight al dente bite. Stir in the cheese and season to taste.

Step 4: Top with the remaining sautéed mushrooms, garnish with parsley, and serve with more grated cheese, if desired.

Source:Love and Lemons - Mushroom Risotto

Margherita Pizza

  • Time: 45 mins
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Water, heated to 110 degrees (very warm, almost too hot for comfort) 1 cup
Fine salt 1 tsp
Parmesan 1/4 cup
Whole wheat flour 2 ¾ cups (345 grams)
Rapid-rise or instant yeast 1 packet, 2 ¼ teaspoons
Honey or sugar 1 tbsp
Extra virgin olive oil 1 tbsp
Tomato Sauce 1 cup
Shredded low-moisture part-skim mozzarella cheese 3 cups
Basil To garnish

Step 1: Preheat oven to 500 degrees Fahrenheit with a rack in the upper third of the oven.

Step 2: Whisk water, oil, honey and yeast in a liquid measuring cup or small bowl. Allow yeast to proof for 5 minutes. It should puff up some by then.

Step 3: Pulse flour, Parmesan, and salt in food processor until combined. While running the food processor, slowly pour in the water mixture and process until a shaggy ball forms, about 1 minute.

Step 4: Dump the dough onto a floured work surface and quickly knead dough a few times until it comes together. Halve the dough.

Step 5: On a floured surface, use a rolling pin to roll dough into two rounds about 11 inches in diameter. For best results, roll the dough out about as thin as reasonably possible. Aim for even thickness rather than a perfectly round shape.

Step 6: Carefully lift and transfer one of the rounds onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the outer 1-inch of the dough with a light coating of olive oil. Add half of the pizza sauce or crushed tomatoes (crush the tomatoes over the sink to get out as much liquid as possible). Sprinkle with half of the cheese and any other toppings you’d like to add.

Step 7: Bake on the top rack until the crust and cheese are lightly golden, for about 12 minutes. Repeat with the remaining dough, then slice and serve. Leftover pizza will keep well in the refrigerator for about 4 days.

Source: Cookie and Kate - Margharita Pizza

Tiramisu

  • Time: 12 hours
  • Serves: 12
Ingredients Quantity
Very strongly prepared espresso, warm or room temperature 1 cup
Ladyfingers 45
Mascarpone, cold 16 ounces
Dark rum 2 tbsp
Pasteurized eggs 4 large
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup
Heavy cream or heavy whipping cream 2 cups
Pure vanilla extract 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/8 tsp
Cocoa Powder As desired

Step 1: You need a large 9×13-inch baking pan/dish with at least a 12-14 cup capacity. Set aside and have ready to go!

Step 2: You will form 2 layers each of dipped ladyfingers and mascarpone cream. Let’s begin with the 1st layer of ladyfingers. Whisk the espresso and together in a shallow bowl. One at a time, quickly dip each side of the ladyfinger into the espresso mixture. You don’t want to over-saturate the ladyfinger with liquid because then the ladyfingers will taste soggy. Just a quick dip on each side. Arrange the dipped ladyfingers in the baking pan to make one solid layer. If needed, cut some ladyfingers to fill in any empty spaces. Reserve remaining espresso mixture and ladyfingers for another layer.

Step 3: With a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle or whisk attachment, beat the mascarpone and rum together on medium speed in a large bowl for a few seconds, just to combine. Do not overmix. Set aside.

Step 4: If you have a double boiler, use it in this step. If you don’t, place a heatproof bowl over a small pot of simmering water over medium-low heat. Don’t let the water touch the bottom of the bowl. Using a whisk or eggbeater (I simply use a whisk), whisk the egg yolks and 1/4 cup (50g) of granulated sugar together until light and foamy, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat and immediately pour into the mascarpone mixture. Beat on medium speed until combined.

Step 5: With a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment, beat the heavy cream and vanilla extract together on medium-high speed until medium peaks form, about 3 minutes. Medium peaks are between soft/loose peaks and stiff peaks and are the perfect consistency for the tiramisu cream. Fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture.

Step 6:With a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a clean whisk attachment, beat the egg whites and salt together on medium-high speed until foamy, about 1 minute. Increase to high speed and slowly pour in the remaining 1/4 cup (50g) of sugar. Beat until stiff peaks form, about 4-5 minutes. (Do not over-beat as the egg whites will become dry.) Fold into the mascarpone cream.

Step 7: Spread half of the mascarpone cream evenly over bottom layer of ladyfingers. An offset spatula helps spread it neatly and evenly.

Step 8: Dip remaining ladyfingers into remaining espresso mixture and arrange one-by-one on top of the mascarpone layer. Gently press each down so they are nice and compact. Using an offset spatula, spread remaining mascarpone mixture evenly on top. If you’re using a large enough pan, it should all fit (but it will definitely tower high!). Refrigerate uncovered for 2-3 hours.

Step 9: After a couple hours in the refrigerator, sift or strain a dense layer of cocoa powder all over the top. Wipe the rim of the baking dish. Cover with plastic wrap or aluminum foil, then refrigerate for at least 8-9 more hours and up to 1 day.

Step 10: Using a sharp knife, slice chilled tiramisu into servings. Wipe knife clean between cuts. A small metal serving spatula is helpful for removing the slices.

Step 11: Cover leftover tiramisu and store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Source: Sally's Baking Addiction - Tiramisu

Classic Almond Biscotti

  • Time: 1 hour 20 mins
  • Serves: 25
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 2 1/4 cup
Caster Suger 3/4 cup
Eggs 2 units
Zest of lemon 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Vanilla extract 1/2 tsp
Almonds unblanched raw, whole 1 cup
Salt 1/2 tsp

Step 1: Start by preheating the oven at 350°F (180°C). Cover baking tray with parchment paper.

Step 2: First you will beat two large eggs and the sugar. Next you’ll add the vanilla. If you are using other extracts, such as almond extract, mix them in now. This is also when you will want to add citrus zest such as orange or lemon zest.

Step 3: In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder and salt. Add the dry ingredients to the wet and mix until combined.

Step 4: Finally, stir in any mix-ins the almonds in this case, or any other ingredients you want to use.

Step 5: For this quantity you can shape the dough into a log shape. Place the logs on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Press down with your hands to flatten the logs, you want them to be 3/4-inch thick to ensure even cooking. You can also use your hands to smooth and flatten the edges of the dough logs.

Step 6: Position oven rack in the middle of the oven and bake the dough logs for 20-25 minutes at 350 degrees, until they are lightly golden. The center will be slightly soft (see notes). At this point lower the oven temperature to to 325°F (175°C).

Step 7: Place the cooked log on a cutting board. Slice the logs on the diagonal, pressing a sharp knife straight down into the dough.

Step 8: Place the cookies back on the baking tray, cut side up. Bake the biscotti a second time, for 12-16 minutes, until golden brown and crisp.

Source: Italian Kitchen Confessions - Biscotti

Gelato

  • Time: 20-25
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Whole Milk 4 cups
Vanilla bean paste 1 tsp
Granulated sugar 1 cup
Cornstarch 5 tsp
Fine grain sea salt 1/4 tsp

Step 1: Place 3 cups of the milk along with the vanilla bean paste in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Slowly, gently, bring just to a simmer.

Step 2: In the meantime, place the remaining cup of milk in a large glass measuring cup (or equivalent), whisk in the sugar, then whisk in the cornstarch. Set aside.

Step 3: As the milk comes to a simmer, remove from heat, and pour the cornstarch mixture into the milk. Place back on medium heat and stir, stir, stir, until things come back up to a simmer and thicken - 10 minutes or so. The goal here is to avoid scorching the milk on the bottom of the pan. You want the mixture thicker than a runny milkshake, but much thinner than a frosty one. Remove from heat, stir in the salt, taste and adjust with more if needed.

Step 4: Strain the mixture into a mixing bowl, let it cool for 20 minutes or so, then place in the refrigerator for a few hours until completely chilled. Don’t short-cut this step, get your mixture as cold as possible.

Step 5:Churn the gelato per your machine’s manufacturer instructions, and serve immediately. Or, you can transfer to a container and place in a freezer for a few hours for a more structured gelato.

Source: 101 Cookbooks - Gelato

Panna Cotta

  • Time: 15 mins plus chilling
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Gelatine 2 1/4 sheets
Milk 150 ml
Double cream 400 ml
Caster sugar 60 g
Vanilla pod 1
Fresh strawberries To serve
Strawberry compote To serve

Step 1: Add the sheets of gelatine to a bowl of cold water and soak for 5 mins.

Step 2: Pour the milk and cream into a saucepan with the sugar and vanilla seeds (to scrape the seeds out of the pod, use the back of a knife). Stir to combine and bring to a simmer, then remove from the heat. Take the gelatine out of the cold water and squeeze out the excess, then add to the milk mixture. Stir until completely dissolved. Tip into four ramekins and place in the fridge to set for at least a couple of hours.

Step 3: To serve, turn each ramekin upside-down onto a serving plate. If the panna cotta won't drop out, carefully dip the ramekin in a bowl of warm water to loosen it. Serve with a drizzle of strawberry compote and sliced fresh strawberries.

Source: Good Food - Panna Cotta
